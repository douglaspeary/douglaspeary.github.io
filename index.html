About Rev. Douglas Kenneth Peary

Douglas Kenneth Peary is a Unitarian Universalist (UU) minister and author whose work centers on religious humanism and the lives of influential humanist thinkers. Born on November 14, 1942, in Washburn, Maine, he was raised in a Conservative Baptist Fundamentalist Christian household on a potato farm. His spiritual journey led him from fundamentalist Christianity to Unitarian Universalism, and eventually to becoming a certified Humanist Celebrant in 1980. He earned his Master of Divinity from Starr King School for Religious Leadership in Berkeley, California, and was ordained as a UU minister in 1979.

Professionally, Peary served as a Federal Labor Investigator with the National Labor Relations Board for 30 years. In 1989, he co-founded the Humanists of Connecticut, where he held leadership roles including president and vice president. His personal life includes marriage to Joyce Lee Martin in 1972, with whom he had two children, Brett and Brita. After Joyce's passing in 1996, he later married Gabriella, a retired educator, in 2008.

Throughout his career, Rev. Peary has authored and edited several books that explore humanist philosophy and biographies of prominent humanist figures. His work aims to make humanist ideas accessible and engaging to readers of all ages, from teenagers to senior citizens. His most notable work includes the "Humanist Heroes" series and the compilation of John Hassler Dietrich's teachings, a key figure in religious humanism.
